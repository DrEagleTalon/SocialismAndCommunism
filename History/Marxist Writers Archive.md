#### [International Working-men's Association](https://www.marxists.org/history/international/iwma/index.htm)  
(First International)

The founders of Marxism, Marx and Engels, participated in the “International Workingmen's Association” from 1864 to 1872, where they found their first base of support and a connection with the workers' movement. Based in London, the International found supporteres across Europe and in the U.S.A.

#### [The Socialist International](https://www.marxists.org/history/international/social-democracy/index.htm)  
(Second International)

In the 1880s, militant workers' movements grew up in all the capitalist countries. Marxists built powerful social-democratic parties which gave political leadership to these movements and transformed Marxism into a worldwide, mass movement.

___

#### [The Comintern](https://www.marxists.org/history/international/comintern/index.htm)

In 1919, the Bolsheviks held a conference attended by revolutionaries from every corner of the world, and established the Communist International (Comintern); soon there were Communist Parties in every country, drawing the most militant workers to the Bolsheviks. After its dissolution in 1943,  the various parties that had made it up kept in touch through the Information Bureau of the Communist and Workers' Parties (Cominform), and through various international conferences.

#### [Marxist Humanism](https://www.marxists.org/subject/humanism/index.htm)

#### [The French Revolution](https://www.marxists.org/history/france/revolution/index.htm)

The leaders of the French Revolution were the first to develop modern social theory and laid the basis for the modern socialism. Rousseau traced the origins of inequality to private property, and Babeuf is credited with being the first Communist. The socialist ideas from the French Revolution are one of the sources of Marxism.

___

#### [Utopian Socialism](https://www.marxists.org/subject/utopian/index.htm)

#### [Political Economy](https://www.marxists.org/subject/economy/index.htm)

Political Economy grew out of moral philosophy in the late 18th century as a new and distinct branch of science, dedicated to understanding how people can live. The critical study of the political economists absorbed much of Karl Marx's life.

___

[Adam Smith](https://www.marxists.org/reference/archive/smith-adam/index.htm) ![*](https://www.marxists.org/archive/images/red-star.jpg) (1723-1790)  
Originally a moral philosopher, became the greatest of the British political economists; first to develop a labour theory of value.  
\[[Biography](https://www.marxists.org/glossary/people/s/m.htm#smith-adam)\]

[Thomas Malthus](https://www.marxists.org/reference/subject/economics/malthus/index.htm) (1766-1834)  
British political economists who theorised economic basis for development of society, and infamous for his reactionary theory of population.  
\[[Biography](https://www.marxists.org/glossary/people/m/a.htm#malthus-thomas)\]

[John Stuart Mill](https://www.marxists.org/reference/archive/mill-john-stuart/index.htm) (1806-1873)  
Contemporary and opponent of Karl Marx, English liberal theorist, early positivist and a Utilitarian in ethics.  
\[[Biography](https://www.marxists.org/glossary/people/m/i.htm#mill-john-stuart)\]

[Marxist Political Economy](https://www.marxists.org/subject/economy/postmarx.htm) 1887-1995

[Bernice Shoul](https://www.marxists.org/archive/shoul/index.htm) (1920-1977)  
U.S. Marxist Political Economist.  
\[[Biography](https://www.marxists.org/glossary/people/s/h.htm#shoul-bernice)\]

[David Yaffe](https://www.marxists.org/subject/economy/authors/yaffed/index.htm)      
English Marxist Political Economist.

[Frederick Taylor](https://www.marxists.org/reference/subject/economics/taylor/index.htm) (1856-1915)  
American management theorist who invented “scientific management.”  
\[[Biography](https://www.marxists.org/glossary/people/t/a.htm#taylor)\]

[John Hobson](https://www.marxists.org/archive/hobson/index.htm) (1858-1940) 15+  
English, socialist economist who was a major theorist of imperialism, was somewhat of a centrist in politics.  
\[[Biography](https://www.marxists.org/glossary/people/t/a.htm#taylor)\]

[J. M. Keynes](https://www.marxists.org/reference/subject/economics/keynes/index.htm) ![*](https://www.marxists.org/archive/images/red-star.jpg) (1883-1946)  
British political economist who developed the theory of the welfare state & macroeconomic control of unemployment by public spending.  
\[[Biography](https://www.marxists.org/glossary/people/k/e.htm#keynes-john-maynard)\]

[Classics of Political Economy](https://www.marxists.org/reference/subject/economics/index.htm) 1673-1936

#### [Natural Science](https://www.marxists.org/subject/science/index.htm)

Marxists have always taken a keen interest in the development of the natural and social sciences and the philosophical problems arising out of science. Even scientists who have had conservative political views have contributed to revolutionary ideas.

___

[Charles Darwin](https://www.marxists.org/reference/archive/darwin/works/origins/index.htm) (1809-1882)  
English biologist who formulated the idea of evolution of species by natural selection.  
\[[Biography](https://www.marxists.org/glossary/people/d/a.htm#darwin-charles)\]

[Lewis Henry Morgan](https://www.marxists.org/reference/archive/morgan-lewis/index.htm) (1818-1881)  
Anthropologist who formulated the idea of development of human society through definite stages corresponding to evolution of the forces of production.  
\[[Biography](https://www.marxists.org/glossary/people/m/o.htm#morgan-henry)\]

[Sigmund Freud](https://www.marxists.org/archive/freud/index.htm) (1856-1939)  
Psychologist, founder of psychoanalysis.  
\[[Biography](https://www.marxists.org/glossary/people/f/r.htm#freud-sigmund)\]

[Albert Einstein](https://www.marxists.org/reference/archive/einstein/index.htm) (1879-1955)  
Discoverer of Theory of Relativity and the Quantum nature of energy; devoted his life to fight for peace and world government.  
\[[Biography](https://www.marxists.org/glossary/people/e/i.htm#einstein-albert)\]
